Managing Site Administration Within Your Organization
Each organization registered in the CTRP system can have one primary site administrator who is responsible for reviewing and updating user access to trials owned by the organization, and for assigning site administrator status to other users in the organization. You can request site administration authority from the CTRO when creating or updating a CTRP Registration Site account. See Step 10 in Creating New CTRP Registration Site Accounts.
You cannot request site administrator status if an administrator is already assigned to your organization. To display the administrator(s) currently assigned to your organization, below the Organization Affiliation field on the My Account page, click View Admins.
The relationships between administrators and trials in a single organization are as follows:
•
An organization can have the following:
•
One original site administrator
•
Many appointed site administrators
•
Many trials
•
Many trial submitters
•
Many trial owners
•
A trial can have the following:
•
One submitter
•
One organization affiliation
•
Many trial owners
•
Many site administrators
•
A site administrator can do the following:
•
Assign and remove trial ownership
•
Grant and revoke administrative rights to other users in the organization
•
Revoke own administrative rights
Site administrators can view and manage only those trials within their own organization.
