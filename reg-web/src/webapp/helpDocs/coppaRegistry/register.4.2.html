Registering Non-Proprietary Trials in the CTRP Registration Site
The CTRP Registration Site provides a user-friendly interface through which you can register new non-proprietary and proprietary trials; and amend or update existing trials in the CTRP system. Additionally, when your registration is verified, you will receive a Trial Summary Report—a system-generated report that contains all your abstracted data—in both MS Word and XML formats. The later is formatted to facilitate the registration of your trials with the Library of Medicine's ClinicalTrials.gov database.
While you can register new proprietary and non-proprietary interventional trials, this section provides information on registering single non-proprietary trials. For information on registering multiple trials, see Registering Multiple Trials in a Batch. For information on registering proprietary files, see Registering Proprietary Trials in the CTRP Registration Site.
You are required to provide information for all fields marked with an asterisk (*).
How to Register Trials
1.
On the navigation pane on the left side of the page, click Register Trial.
The Select Trial Type window appears.
2.
Click No.
The Register Trial page appears.
3.
Type the appropriate information in the text fields, or select options from the drop-down lists as appropriate according to the detailed instructions provided for each of the following sections of the page:
•
Completing the Trial Details Section
•
Completing the Lead Organization/Principal Investigator Section
•
Completing the Sponsor/Responsible Party Section
•
Completing the Summary 4 Information Section
•
Completing the NIH Grant Information Section
•
Completing the Trial Status/Dates Section
•
Completing the IND/IDE Information Section
•
Completing the Trial Related Documents Section
4.
Scroll to the bottom of the Register Trial page, and click Review Trial.
The system checks for errors and missing information and displays the results in a message at the top of the Review Trial Details page. Indicators mark specific fields that you must complete or correct in order to submit the trial.
5.
Correct any errors if indicated, and repeat the previous step as many times as necessary until the trial is error-free.
The Review Trial Details page is read-only. To make changes to the trial data, follow the instructions in Editing Trial Details.
6.
Click Submit.
The system sends you an email message to acknowledge that the trial has been submitted. After submission, no other users can see the trial information you provided until the information has been validated. If the trial is rejected at validation, the system alerts you via a rejection message. Once validated, the trial you submitted is ready for abstraction by an NCI Clinical Trials Reporting Office (CTRO) specialist.
