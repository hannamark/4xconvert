Recording Trial Statuses/Dates
Trial status refers to the current stage or state of a clinical trial or study relative to other stages and its ability to enroll participants/patients.
Valid trial statuses used in CTRP are mapped to ClinicalTrials.gov-defined recruitment values as listed in the table below. ClinicalTrials.gov status definitions are provided in Site trial status descriptions.

The diagram below illustrates the relationships between CTRP trial statuses. You must record the statuses in the order shown. For example, you cannot record the Temporarily Closed to Accrual status without previously having recorded an Active status.
There are two exceptions to the rule, indicated in the diagram by red arrows. You can transition between the following statuses:
•
From In Review to Active, without first recording an Approved status. This allows you to submit an amendment when the Approved status and Active status occur in the same day.
•
From Active to Closed to Accrual and Intervention, without first recording a Closed to Accrual status
Valid dates for a given trial status depend on the other values you have entered, and whether those dates are Actual (current, or past) or Anticipated (future).
The following diagram illustrates these rules. Line A represents the flow of statuses over time. Time between statuses is not important. Line B represents the Trial Start dates relative to the statuses, and Line C represents the same for Primary Completion dates. Closely-spaced dashed lines indicate dates that are Anticipated. Widely-spaced dashed lines indicate dates that are Actual.
