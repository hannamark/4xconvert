Registering Non-Proprietary Trials
This section provides the main steps for registering single non-proprietary trials. For information on registering multiple trials, see Registering Multiple Trials in a Batch. For information on registering proprietary trials, see Registering Proprietary Trials.
If you cannot complete the registration of a non-proprietary trial in one CTRP Registration Site session, you can save a draft of the trial details you have completed. Later you can return to complete the registration in another session. This does not apply to proprietary trials.
Be sure to provide information for all fields marked with an asterisk (*). The trial details you are asked to provide vary depending on the ownership and category of the trial you are registering. For details, see Viewing Trial Details.
Following are the major steps for registering a trial. Detailed steps for registering non-proprietary trials are provided in the sections listed in Step 3.
How to Register Trials
1.
On the navigation pane on the left side of the page, click Register Trial.
The Select Trial Type window appears.
2.
Next to Is your trial proprietary, click No, and then click Submit.
The Register Trial page appears.
3.
If you require an XML document to register your trial with ClinicalTrials.gov, next to XML required?, select the Yes option. If you are not going to submit your trial to ClinicalTrials.gov, select the No option. The option you select here will dictate which other sections you will be required to complete. For example, if you select No, you will not be required to complete responsible party and regulatory information.If you select Yes, NCI will be added as a collaborator to the Funding Source.Type the appropriate information in the text fields, or select options from the drop-down lists as appropriate according to the detailed instructions provided for each of the following sections of the page:

4.
Do one of the following to continue:
•
If you have provided, at the minimum, both the Lead Organization and Lead Organization Trial Identifier, you can save a draft of the record so that you can complete the registration at another time. To do so, click Save as Draft.
The system saves your draft, assigns it a unique ID (for tracking purposes), and sends you an email message confirming that the information has been saved. You can end your CTRP Registration Site and retrieve your draft later to complete the registration. The system stores the draft for 30 days.
Trial-related documents you may have attached during the current session will not be saved as part of the draft record.
- or-
•
To continue with the trial registration, scroll to the bottom of the Register Trial page, and click Review Trial.
The system checks for errors and missing information and displays the results in a message at the top of the Review Trial Details page. Indicators mark specific fields that you must complete or correct in order to submit the trial.
5.
Correct any errors if indicated, and repeat the previous step as many times as necessary until the trial is error-free.
The Review Trial Details page is read-only. To make changes to the trial data, follow the instructions in Editing Trial Details.
6.
Click Submit.
The system sends you an email message to acknowledge that the trial has been submitted. After submission, no other users can see the trial information you provided until the information has been validated. If the trial is rejected at validation, the system alerts you via a rejection message. Once validated, the trial you submitted is ready for abstraction by an NCI Clinical Trials Reporting Office (CTRO) specialist.

Editing Trial Details
You can edit trial details after you have reviewed them at any time before you submit the trial to the CTRP. If you want to edit a trial that you submitted previously, follow the instructions in Updating and Amending Registered Trials.
