Creating and Submitting New Accrual Submission Records
The CTRP Accrual site enables you to record accrual data associated with a particular complete1 trial registered with the CTRP. You must select a trial to work on before you can create an accrual or study subject record.
You can not create a new accrual record for a trial that has an accrual record with the “opened” status. For status information, see Reviewing Accrual Records.
How to Create New Accrual Submission Records
1.
Navigate to the appropriate trial and click the NCI Trial Identifier. For details, see Searching for Trials.
The Accrual Submissions page appears and displays any accrual submissions that may have been submitted previously, and/or which are open but not yet submitted.
2.
Click Add New.
The New Accrual Submission Page appears.
3.
In the Submission Title field, type an appropriate title for the accrual record. This title will appear in the Label column of the accrual record.
4.
Enter the submission cutoff date in the Submission Cut-off Date field. See Selecting and Entering Dates.
Note:
When submitting the accrual record, the cut-off date must be the current date or some time in the past. It must also be the same as, or after, the Cut-off-Date of the previous submission for the same trial.
5.
In the Description field, type a description of the accrual information you are submitting.
6.
Click Save.
7.
The Accrual Submissions page displays the record you just created.Optionally, to preview the information you want to submit, click the link in the Label column.
The Accrual Submission Details page displays the information you provided.
8.
To continue with the submission, click Back.
9.
If all the information is complete, on the Accrual Submissions page, click the Submit icon.
10.
On the pop-up window, click OK to confirm your submission.
The Accrual Submissions page displays your new record. The submission date and your user name appear along with the record, and the status is changed to “Submitted”.
11.
Optionally, to create another record, click Add New, and repeat the steps above.
1
In previous versions of Accrual, a complete trial was referred to as non-proprietary. Complete trials, categorized by Summary 4 funding, include National, Externally Peer-Reviewed, and Institutional trials.
