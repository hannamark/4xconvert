Adding Study Subjects
 
CTRP Accrual enables you to search for and select diseases and conditions from a snapshot of the PDQ (Physician Data Query) database and link them to a trial. PDQ is an online database developed and maintained by the National Cancer Institute. You can find more specific information about PDQ at http://cancer.gov/cancer_information/pdq/.
Note:
PDQ terminology snapshot may be extended by new terms validated in EVS (Enterprise Vocabulary Service).
 
You are required to complete all fields marked with an asterisk (*).
How to Add Study Subjects
1.
Navigate to the trial you want to work with by following the instructions in Selecting Listed Trials on page 15 or Searching for Trials on page 16, and then click the corresponding NCI Trial Identifier link.
2.
On the Navigation pane, click Study Subject Search.
The Search Study Subject page appears.
3.
Click Add New Study Subject.
THe Add Study Subject page appears.
4.
Type or select the appropriate demographic information as described in the table below.

5.
Do one of the following to record the date that the subject was registered for the trial:
•
In the Registration Date field type the date in the format mm/dd/yyyy.
- or -
•
Click the calendar icon () and select the date from the pop-up calendar.
The registration date must precede the accrual cut-off date.
6.
For United States study subjects only, from the Study Subject Method of Payment drop-down list, select the appropriate payment method.
7.
Next to the Disease field, click Look Up to select the subject’s disease.
8.
To search the PDQ database for the name of the disease or condition being studied, next to the Disease field, click Look Up.
The Search Diseases Window appears.
9.
In the Disease Name field, type part or all of the disease/condition being studied.
10.
Type as many letters of the disease/condition name as possible to reduce the number of search results. The system adds wildcards on both sides of the search string (the series of letters you type) for you implicitly. You can type wildcard symbols (*) between characters of the string as necessary.To include synonyms in your search in addition to preferred names, select the Include Synonym check box.
11.
To limit your search to exact matches only, select the Exact Match Only check box.
12.
Click Search.
The system searches for the disease/condition from both preferred names and synonyms in the database, and returns a list of results.
Note: The system will not return any results if you have typed too few charac­ters in the Disease Name field or have typed a disease/condition that has numerous sub-concepts.
13.
Scroll through the list (if needed) to locate the disease/condition being studied.
Some records in the results list may not be suitable for selection because they are not high enough in the concept hierarchy.
14.
If the disease/condition being studied appears in the list, click Select, and proceed to Step 11.
15.
If the disease/condition being studied does not appear in the list, search for it in the Enterprise Vocabulary Services (EVS) repository in the NCI Bio Portal (http://bioportal.nci.nih.gov/ncbo/faces/pages/search.xhtml).
a.
If EVS lists the disease/condition, contact Protocol Abstraction Application Support to request that it be added to the list in Protocol Abstraction.
b.
If EVS does not list the disease/condition, contact EVS Application Support to request that it be added to the repository as a new concept
16.
Navigate to the appropriate disease, and click Select.
The system populates the Disease field for you.
17.
From the Participating Site drop-down list, select the appropriate site.
18.
Click Save.
19.
The study subject record appears in the list of study subjects.
The Record Status remains pending.
