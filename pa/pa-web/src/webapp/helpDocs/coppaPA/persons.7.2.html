Looking Up Registered Persons
The procedure for searching for the name of a person registered in the Clinical Trials Reporting Program is the same throughout the Protocol Abstraction application, whether you are searching for principle investigators, responsible parties, or other roles. To link a person to a role, you select the personâ€™s record from a list of search results.
Note:
The procedure below uses the principle investigator role on the Trial Details page as an example.
How to Look Up Registered Persons
1.
On the Trial Details page, navigate to the Lead Organization/Principle Investigator section.
2.
Next to the Principle Investigator field, click Look Up.
The Search Persons window appears.
3.
Provide as much information as you can about your investigator.
You can type one or more initial characters in either of the Name fields to narrow the search results, but do not use wildcard symbols (*). For example, to search for an investigator whose last name is Slocum, type S, Sl, or Slo in the Last Name field.
Note:
You must enter search criteria in at least one field.
4.
Click Search.
The Search Persons window displays the results of your search.
If your principal investigator is not listed, you may have searched too narrowly (that is, you may have provided too much information about the person). If the list of results is very long and contains many names that are similar to yours, you can narrow your search by providing more information. Refer to Step 5 for instructions.
