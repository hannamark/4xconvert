screening
Protocol designed to assess or examine methods of identifying a condition (or risk factors for a condition) in people who are not yet known to have the condition (or risk factor).
supportive care
Protocol designed to evaluate one or more interventions where the primary intent is to maximize comfort, minimize side effects or mitigate against a decline in the subject's health or function. In general, supportive care interventions are not intended to cure a disease.

Name of primary organization that oversees implementation of study and is responsible for data analysis.

treatment
Protocol designed to evaluate one or more interventions for treating a disease, syndrome or condition.

Code for a clinical trial that represents a distinguishable part or stage in a series of events or in a process of development. Clinical trials are broken into three or four phases.
trial status
The current status of a clinical study in relation to the ability to enroll participants/patients.
trial type
Nature of the trial. Identifies a clinical study by product, procedure, or method tested. The type of clinical trial performed, e.g. efficacy, safety.
