Abstracting Trial Status Information
This section pertains to complete trials only. Trial status is an indication of the progress of the study itself, and not the status of Protocol Abstraction processing. Trial Start Dates and Primary Completion Dates are related to the current trial status. See Trial Processing Statuses and the status rules and relationships information in Validating Trial Status Details.
Before you begin abstracting or validating any trial, be sure you check it out first. Otherwise you will not be able to modify any trial data.
How to Abstract Trial Status Information
1.
On the navigation pane, click Trial Status.
The Trial Status section appears.
2.
Optionally, to view a history of the trial’s status milestones, click History in the upper right corner of the Trial Status section.
The Trial Status History appears in a new browser window.
3.
Select or enter the appropriate information in the text fields and drop-down lists. Fields are described in the following table. All fields are required.
Field Label
Description/Instructions
Current Trial Status
Select the trial’s accrual status from the drop-down list. Valid values are as follows:
•
In Review. Trial has been submitted to the review board for approval. Board approval is pending.
•
Disapproved. Trial has been disapproved by the review board upon submission and will not be re-submitted.
•
Approved. Trial has been approved by the review board. Study activation preparation has begun.
•
Active. Trial is open for accrual.
•
Closed to Accrual. Trial has been closed to participant accrual. Participants are still receiving treatment/intervention.
•
Closed to Accrual and Intervention. Trial has been closed to participant accrual. No participants are receiving treatment/intervention, but participants are still being followed according to the primary objectives of the study.
•
Temporarily Closed to Accrual. Trial is temporarily not accruing.
•
Temporarily Closed to Accrual and Intervention. Trial is temporarily not accruing. Participants are not receiving intervention.
•
Withdrawn. Trial has been withdrawn from development and review.
•
Administratively Complete. Trial has been completed prematurely (for example, due to poor accrual, insufficient drug supply, IND closure, etc.
•
Complete. Trial has been closed to accrual; participants have completed treatment/intervention, and the study has met its primary objectives.
Current Trial Status Date*
Enter the date on which the current trial status became effective. See Selecting and Entering Dates and Rules for Status/Dates relationships.
Why Study Stopped
If you selected the Administratively Complete, Withdrawn, or Temporarily Closed to Accrual status, type the reason why the study has ended or is not currently accruing.
Trial Start Date*
1.
Enter the date on which the trial started, or is expected to start. See Selecting and Entering Dates.
2.
Indicate whether the start date is the one on which the trial is expected to start, or the date on which it actually started by selecting Anticipated or Actual. See Rules for Status/Dates relationships.
Primary Completion Date*
1.
Enter the date on which the trial ended, or is expected to end. See Selecting and Entering Dates.
2.
Indicate whether the start date is the one on which the trial is expected to start, or the date on which it actually started, by selecting Anticipated or Actual. See Rules for Status/Dates relationships.
4.
To save the details you have abstracted, click Save.
