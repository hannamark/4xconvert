Abstracting Regulatory Information
Regulatory information includes human subject safety review and IND/IDE data. The information you are required to provide depends on the oversight authority and other indicators, as noted in the instructions that follow.
How to Abstract Regulatory Information
1.
On the navigation sidebar, click Regulatory Information.
The Regulatory Information page appears.
2.
In the Trial Oversight Authority Country field, validate the name of the country that is responsible for oversight of the trial. If the country name is not valid, select the correct country from the drop-down list.
3.
In the Trial Oversight Authority Organization Name field, select the name of the organization that is responsible for oversight of the trial from the drop-down list.
4.
Indicate whether the trial is regulated by the FDA by selecting Yes or No (the default value) in the FDA Regulated Intervention Indicator drop-down list. If you selected Yes, proceed to the next step. Otherwise, skip to Step 6.
If the trial is interventional and you indicated that it is regulated by the FDA , the Regulatory Information section displays fields for Section 801 and data monitoring commitee information. You are required to complete these fields.
5.
Indicate whether the FDA-regulated interventional trial is an applicable trial as defined in US Public Law 110-85, Title VIII, Section 801 by selecting Yes or No (the default value) in the Section 801 Indicator drop-down list.
If you indicated that the trial is applicable under Section 801, the Regulatory Information section displays an additional field for IND/IDE indication.You are required to complete this field.
6.
Indicate whether the trial involves an Investigational Device Exemption (IDE) or Investigational New Drug (IND) by selecting Yes or No (the default value) in the IND/IDE Indicator drop-down list. If you selected Yes, proceed to the next step. Otherwise, skip to Step 8.
If you indicated that the trial trial involves an IDE or IDE, the Regulatory Information section displays an additional field for Delayed Posting indication. You are required to complete this field.
7.
For IND trials, indicate whether the release of trial information on ClinicalTrials.gov is being delayed until after an interventional device has been approved or cleared by selecting Yes or No (the default value) in the Delayed Posting Indicator drop-down list.
8.
Indicate whether a data monitoring committee has been appointed for this trial by selecting Yes or No (the default value) in the Data Monitoring Committee Appointed Indicator drop-down list.
9.
To save the details you have abstracted, click Save.
10.
Do one of the following to continue:
•
To review the data you provided on the previous page, click Back.
- or -
•
To proceed with the abstraction and move to the next page, click Next.
