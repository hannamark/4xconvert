Overview of the Protocol Abstraction Service
Abstractions are the summary, explicit, derived, and meta- data that describe the pertinent details of a given protocol. The Protocol Abstraction service (PAS) provides a uniform means for accessing, managing, querying, linking, and validating various protocol abstractions within the context of protocol-driven clinical trials.
Protocols (and their various abstractions) form the essential cornerstone of clinical research. However, currently there is no standard for recording the collection of concepts, attributes, and relationships that define all possible protocols. And, a given protocol may be abstracted many times during its lifecycle. PAS offers the functionality necessary to manage, link, query, and validate multiple abstractions of a single protocol without losing either the level of detail of a particular abstraction or the unique identity of both the abstraction itself and the protocol from which it was derived.
The Protocol Abstraction Service (PAS) provides a set of interfaces for managing the input, output, correlation, and other administrative management functions associated with 'abstractions' (i.e. data and meta-data) from/about a given protocol.
In the context of the PAS, the term 'abstraction' is used in the peer-review-journal sense to mean 'to provide a summary and/or essential points of interest of a particular object,' rather than in the object-oriented-modeling sense of meaning 'a process by which data/details are intentionally hidden with the intent of providing a 'higher-level view of a particular object.' Although the two meanings of the word have some overlap around the notion of 'summarizing,' the former use of the word (i.e. as it is used in the PAS context) allows for managing content at various levels of 'abstraction' (in the second sense of the word) for a given protocol including data (explicit and derived), meta-data, and summary data 'abstractions.'
