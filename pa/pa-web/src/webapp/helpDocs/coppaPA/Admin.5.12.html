Abstracting Trial IND/IDE Information
Complete this section only if the protocol includes an Investigational New Drug (IND) or Investigational Device Exemption (IDE) as per US Food and Drug Administration regulations. If the trial was registered without IND/IDE information, you can provide it here.
Some fields in this section are optional, and some may be required only when you have selected certain attributes in preceding fields. For example, if the Board Approval Status is Submitted, exempt, the Board Name, Board Affiliation, and Board Contact fields are mandatory, but the Board Approval Number is optional.

How to Abstract IND/IDE Information
1.
On the navigation pane, click Trial IND/IDE.
The Trial IND/IDE Page appears.
2.
To add an IND/IDE record, click Add.
The IND/IDE Information section displays IND/IDE data fields.
3.
Indicate the type of protocol by selecting either IND or IDE.
4.
Type the IND/IDE number in the field provided.
5.
Select the grantor from the Grantor drop-down list. The following table lists valid grantors:
Valid Grantors
CDER – Center for Drug Evaluation and Research
CBER – Center for Biologics Evaluation and Research
CDRH – Center for Devices and Radiological Health
6.
Select the holder type from the Holder Type drop-down list. The following table lists valid holder types:
Valid IND/IDE Types
Investigator
Organization
Industry
NIH
NCI
7.
Do one of the following:
•
If you selected any value other than NIH nor NCI, proceed to Step 9.
- or -
•
If you selected either NIH or NCI in the previous step, proceed to the next step.
8.
If the holder type is NIH, select the associated NIH institute from the NIH Institute/NCI Division/Program field. The following table lists valid NIH institutes
NIH Institutes
NEI – National Eye Institute
NHLBI – National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute
NHGRI – National Human Genome Research Institute
NIA – National Institute on Aging
NIAAA – National Institute on Alcohol Abuse and Alcoholism
NIAID – National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases
NIAMS – National Institute of Arthritis and Musculoskeletal and Skin Diseases
NIBIB – National Institute of Biomedical Imaging and Bioengineering
NICHD – Eunice Kennedy Shriver National Institute of Child Health and
Human Development
NIDCD – National Institute on Deafness and Other Communication Disorders
NIDCR – National Institute of Dental and Craniofacial Research
NIDDK – National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases
NIDA – National Institute on Drug Abuse
NIEHS – National Institute of Environmental Health Sciences
NIGMS – National Institute of General Medical Sciences
NIMH – National Institute of Mental Health
NINDS – National Institute of Neurological Disorders and Stroke
NINR – National Institute of Nursing Research
NLM – National Library of Medicine
CIT – Center for Information Technology
CSR – Center for Scientific Review
FIC – John E. Fogarty International Center for Advanced Study in the Health Sciences
NCCAM – National Center for Complementary and Alternative Medicine
NCMHD – National Center on Minority Health and Health Disparities
NCRR – National Center for Research Resources
CC – NIH Clinical Center
OD – Office of the Director
9.
Indicate whether or not the drug or device is available outside any clinical trial protocol by selecting the appropriate Expanded Access Indicator.
10.
If you selected “YES” in the Expanded Access is Indicator field, select the status of the drug or device access from the Expanded Access Status field. The following table lists valid access values
Status
Definition
Available
Expanded access is currently available for this treatment
No longer available
Expanded access was available for this treatment previously but is not currently available and will not be available in the future
Temporarily not available
Expanded access is not currently available for this treatment, but is expected to be available in the future
Approved for marketing
This treatment has been approved for sale to the public
11.
Click Save.
The IND/IDE record you just created is added to the list of existing records.
12.
To make changes to the record, click the Edit icon (pencil) in the Edit column column next to the record you want to modify, and make changes in the form.
13.
To delete the record, click the Delete icon (trash can) in the Delete column.
There may be more than one record in the table. Be sure you select the Edit or Delete icon in the same row as the record you want to modify.
14.
Do one of the following to continue:
•
To add another IND/IDE record, click Add, and complete the steps above.
- or -
•
To review the data you provided on the previous page, click Back.
- or -
•
To proceed with the abstraction and move to the next page, click Next.
