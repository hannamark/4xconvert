Abstracting Interventional Trial Design Details
How to Abstract Interventional Trial Design Details
1.
On the navigation pane, click Design Details.
The Interventional Trial Design – Design Details page appears.
2.
In the Primary Purpose field, select the primary reason for conducting the trial.
CT.GOV does not accept some values that are listed in the Protocol Abstraction. For interventional trials that contain values that CT.GOV does not accept, the system assigns the value of “Other” during abstraction. 3.
If the primary purpose is “Other,” type a detailed description of the trial’s purpose in the text field provided.
4.
In the Trial Phase field, select the current phase of the trial.
CT.GOV does not accept some values that are listed in the Protocol Abstraction. For trials that contain values that CT.GOV does not accept, the system assigns the value of “N/A” during abstraction. 5.
In the Intervention Model drop-down list, select the main study design. The following table lists valid models.
Model
Definition
Single Group
Single arm study
Parallel
Participants are assigned to one of two or more groups in parallel for the duration of the study.
Cross-over
Participants receive one of two alternative interventions during the initial phase of the study and receive the other intervention during the second phase of the study.
Factorial
Two or more interventions, each alone and in combination, are evaluated in parallel against a control group.
6.
In the Number of Arms field, type the number of treatment groups in the trial.
The number of arms you enter in this field must coincide with the number of trial arms on the “Arms” page (see Abstracting Arm Information).
7.
In the Masking field, select the masking type.
The following table lists valid values.
Masking Type
Definition
Open
Single arm study
Single Blind
A study in which one party, either the investigator or participant, is unaware of what medication the participant is taking; also called single-masked study.
Double Blind
Two or more parties are unaware of the intervention assignment.
8.
If the masking is Single Blind, select one masking role. If the masking is Double Blind, select at least two masking roles. Valid masking roles are as follows:
•

•
Investigator
•

•

9.
In the Allocation field, select the means by which participants are assigned to an intervention group.The following table lists valid values.
Allocation Method
Definition
N/A
Single arm study
Randomized Controlled Trial
Participants are assigned to intervention groups by chance.
Non-randomized Trial
Participants are expressly assigned to intervention groups through a non-random method, such as physician choice.
10.
In the Study Classification field, select the type of primary outcome or endpoint that the protocol is designed to evaluate. The following table lists valid values.
Study Classification
Definition
N/A
Not applicable
Safety
Indication that an intervention is safe under conditions of proposed use.
Efficacy
The maximum ability of a drug or treatment to produce a result regardless of dosage. A drug passes efficacy trials if it is effective at the dose tested and against the illness for which it is prescribed. In the procedure mandated by the FDA, Phase II clinical trials gauge efficacy, and Phase III trials confirm it.
Safety/Efficacy
Combined study of safety and efficacy.
Bio-equivalence
Scientific basis for comparing generic and brand name drugs.
Bio-availability
Rate and extent to which a drug is absorbed or otherwise available to the treatment site in the body.
Pharmacokinetics
The action of a drug in the body over a period of time including the process of absorption distribution and localization in tissue, biotransformation, and excretion of the compound.
Pharmacodynamics
Action of drugs in living systems.
Pharmacokinetics/dynamics
Combined study of pharmacokinetics and pharmicodynamics.
11.
In the Target Enrollment field, type the target number of subjects in the study.
12.
Click Save.
