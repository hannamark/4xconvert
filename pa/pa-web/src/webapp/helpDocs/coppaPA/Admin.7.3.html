Abstracting General Trial Details
General trial details include the following main categories of data:
•
ClinicalTrials.gov XML Required indicator (displayed for complete trials only)
•
Trial title
•
Trial description
•
Other identifiers
•
Lead organization/Principal investigator
•
Sponsor Responsible party (complete trials that require an XML document for submission to ClinicalTrials.gov only)
•
Central contact
Unless otherwise noted, you are required to complete all information provided on the General Trial Details page. Most elements are provided via the CTRP Registration Site.
Before you begin abstracting any trial, be sure you check it out first. Otherwise you will not be able to modify any trial data.
How to Abstract General Trial Details
1.
On the navigation pane, click General Trial Details.
The General Trials Details page appears.
2.
Select or enter the appropriate information in the text fields and drop-down lists. Fields are described in the following table. Items with an asterisk are required fields.
Field Label
Trial Category
Description/Instructions
ClinicalTrials.gov XML required?
Complete
Select the appropriate option to indicate whether an XML document is required to register the trial with ClinicalTrials.gov.
Lead Organization Trial Identifier *
Complete and Abbreviated
Type the ID exactly as it appears in the protocol document. For Inter-Group trials, type the Lead Group’s trial number. For multi-site trials that have no assigned single center, use the protocol ID.
Example: NSABP-B-40
NCT Identifier
Complete and Abbreviated
Type the trial identifier assigned by the National Clinical Trial program (Clinical Trials.gov).
Provide the exact number, including the NCT prefix. Example: NCT00012345
CTEP Identifier
Complete
Optionally, in the CTEP Indentifier field, type the trial identifier assigned by the Clinical Therapy Evaluation Program.
DCP Identifier
Complete
Optionally, in the DCP Indentifier fields, type the trial identifier assigned by the Division of Cancer Prevention.
Trial Phase*
Abbreviated
Select the trial phase from the drop-down list. The system links the phase additional qualifier Pilot with the Trial Phase N/A.
Primary Purpose*
Abbreviated
Select the primary purpose of the trial from the drop-down list.
Primary Purpose Description
Abbreviated
Select the primary purpose of the trial from the drop-down list.
Acronym
Complete and Abbreviated
Type the abbreviation (initial letters) by which the trial is known.
Official Title*
Complete and Abbreviated
Ensure that the title is accurate and complete (as it appears in the protocol document). Make any necessary changes or additions
Other Trial Identifier
Complete
Type an additional trial identifier such as unique identifier from other registries, NIH grant numbers, or protocol numbers assigned by the Review Board, and then click Add Other Identifier. Repeat this step for each additional identifier.
The ID you added is displayed in the Other Identifier list
To delete an identifier you have added, in the Action column, click Delete.
Keywords
Complete and Abbreviated
Trial descriptions can contain a keyword or series of keywords that can help to classify the trial.
Lead Organization*
Complete and Abbreviated
Click Look Up Org, and search for the organization as per the instructions in Looking Up Registered Organizations.
Principal* Investigator
Complete
Click Look Up Person, and search for the organization as per the instructions in Looking Up Registered Persons.
3.
Do one of the following to continue:
•
If the trial that you are abstracting is an abbreviated trial, click Save.
- or -
•
If the trial that you are abstracting requires an XML document for submission to ClinicalTrials.gov, complete the Sponsor and Responsible Party (Step 4) and Central Contact (Step 5) sections.
- or -
•
If the trial that you are abstracting does not require an XML document for submission to ClinicalTrials.gov, complete the Central Contact (Step 5) section.
4.
Select or enter the appropriate information in the text fields and drop-down lists. Fields are described in the following table. Items with an asterisk are required fields.
Field Label
Description/Instructions
Sponsor*
Click Look up Sponsor and search for the sponsor as per the instructions in Looking Up Registered Organizations.
Responsible Party*
Indicate the party who is responsible for the trial. Select one of the following options:
•
PI (principal investigator). Primary medical researcher in charge of carrying out a clinical trial's protocol.
- or-
•
Sponsor. Name of primary organization that oversees implementation of study and is responsible for data analysis. For applicable clinical trials, sponsor is defined in 21 CFR 50.3. For further elaboration on the definition of Sponsor with respect to responsible party, see http://prsinfo.clinicaltrials.gov/ElaborationsOnDefinitions.pdf.
Note:
A sponsoring organization may designate the primary investigator or other specific person as the responsible party, or it may designate a generic title or role rather than a person. For example, the sponsor’s generic contact may be “clinical study office,” “director of the clinical study department,” etc.
Responsible Party Contact
If you selected Sponsor above, the Sponsor/Responsible Party sectionexpands to display the Responsible Party Contact and Responsible Party Generic Contact fields.
If the sponsor is a particular person rather than a generic contact, click Look Up Person, and follow the instructions in Looking Up Registered Persons.
Responsible Party Generic Contact
If the sponsor contact is not linked to a particular person, click Look Up Generic Contact, and follow the instructions in Looking Up Generic Contacts to record the responsible contact’s information.
Email Address
If the sponsor contact is not linked to a particular person, type the responsible party’s contact email address.
Each title or role must be unique. You can not create one role with many email addresses and/or phone numbers.
Phone Number
Type the responsible party’s contact phone number. Include phone number extensions where applicable.
You can use spaces or dashes as separators, or no separators at all. See Entering Phone and Fax Numbers.
5.
Select or enter the appropriate central contact information in the text fields and drop-down lists. Fields are described in the following table. All central contact information is optional.
A central contact may be a specific person, or it may be a generic title or role rather than a person. For example, the central contact may be “clinical study office,” “director of the clinical study department,” etc.
Field Label
Description/Instructions
Personal Contact
If the sponsor is a particular person rather than a generic contact, click Look Up Person, and follow the instructions in CTRP Protocol Abstraction Icons.
To remove a contact currently listed in the Central Contact field, click Remove.
Generic Contact
If the sponsor contact is not linked to a particular person, click Look Up Generic Contact, and follow the instructions in Looking Up Generic Contacts to record the responsible contact’s information.
Email Address
Type the contact’s main email address.
Phone Number
Type the contact’s main contact phone number.
