Adding Other Eligibility Criteria
If the trial that you are abstracting uses other criteria in addition to the health, gender, and age of study participants, you can create additional eligibility criteria records in Protocol Abstraction.
How to Add Other Eligibility Criteria
1.
On the navigation pane, click Eligibility Criteria.
The Interventional Trial Design – Eligibility Criteria page appears.
If the Add Other Criteria button is not displayed, complete all fields on the Eligibility page first. To do so, see Abstracting Interventional Trial Eligibility Criteria.
2.
Click Add Other Criteria.
The Add/Edit Eligibility Criteria Page appears.
3.
In the Eligibility Criteria Type field, indicate whether satisfying the criteria qualifies or disqualifies a subject from participation in the trial by selecting Inclusion or Exclusion respectively.
4.
To describe the criteria in free text form, in the Eligibility Criteria Description field, type the details.
For example:
•
Patients with uncontrolled infection.
•
Patients with concurrent severe medical problems unrelated to the malignancy that would significantly limit full compliance with the study or expose the patient to extreme risk or decreased life expectancy.
•
Signed an approved informed consent and authorization permitting release of personal health information.
5.
To build criteria based on measurable values, continue with the next step.
a.
In the Eligibility Criteria Name field, type a name that identifies the criterion.
For example:
•
Systolic blood pressure
•
Total billirubin
•
Absolute neutrophil count
b.
In the Operator field, type the notation that establishes the upper or lower limits of the value.
For example:
•
“=” (equals)
•
“>” (greater than)
•
“<“(less than)
c.
In the Value field, type the number and/or text used for comparison.
For example:
•
1.5 times
•
150
d.
In the Unit field, type the value’s unit of measure.
For example:
•
ULN
•
mm Hg
6.
Click Save.
The new criterion record is displayed on the Eligibility Criteria page.
