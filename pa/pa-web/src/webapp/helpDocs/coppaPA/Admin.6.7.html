Abstracting Sponsors and Responsible Parties
This section pertains only to trials that require an XML document for submission to ClinicalTrials.gov.
A sponsoring organization may designate the primary investigator or other specific person as the responsible party, or it may designate a generic title or role rather than a person. For example, the sponsor’s generic contact may be “clinical study office,” “director of the clinical study department,” etc.
Before you begin abstracting or validating any trial, be sure you check it out first. Otherwise you will not be able to modify any trial data.
How to Abstract Sponsors and Responsible Parties
1.
Click Look up Sponsor and select the appropriate sponsor organization from the list of search results.
2.
Indicate the party who is responsible for the trial. Select one of the following options:
•
PI (principal investigator). Primary medical researcher in charge of carrying out a clinical trial's protocol.
- or-
•
Sponsor. Name of primary organization that oversees implementation of study and is responsible for data analysis. For applicable clinical trials, sponsor is defined in 21 CFR 50.3. For further elaboration on the definition of Sponsor with respect to responsible party, see http://prsinfo.clinicaltrials.gov/ElaborationsOnDefinitions.pdf.
3.
If you selected Sponsor in the previous step, the Sponsor/Responsible Party section expands to display the Responsible Party Contact and Responsible Party Generic Contact fields. If the sponsor is a person rather than a generic contact, next to the Responsible Party Contact field, click Look Up Person, and follow the instructions in Looking Up Registered Persons to record the responsible contact’s information.
4.
If the sponsor contact is not linked to a particular person, next to the Responsible Party Generic Contact field, click Look Up Generic Contact.
The Select Responsible Party Generic Contact window appears.
5.
In the Title field, type the contact’s title or part of the title, and then click Search.
6.
If no results are returned, click Add Generic Contact, and continue with the steps below. Otherwise, skip to Step 8.
The Add Generic Contact window appears.
7.
Type the contact’s title or role, and contact information in the fields provided, and then click Save.
Each title or role must be unique. You can not create one role with many email addresses and/or phone numbers.
The record you created appears in the results table at the bottom of the window.
8.
In the contact record you created or searched for, select the contact’s email address and phone number from the drop-down lists, and then click Select.
Note:
If the correct phone/email address is not listed, contact the PO curator. A PO curator must validate all existing and new phone/email addresses and update the organizational contact record. You can continue abstract­ing the organizational contact after the record has been curated in PO.
9.
The title appears in the Responsible Party Generic Contact field, and the contact’s email address and phone number appear in their respective fields below the title.
10.
In the Email Address and Phone Number fields, type the responsible party’s contact email address and phone number. You can use spaces or dashes as separators, or no separators at all in the Phone Number fields. Include phone number extensions where applicable.
